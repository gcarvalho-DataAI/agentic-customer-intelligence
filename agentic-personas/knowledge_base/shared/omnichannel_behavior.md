# Omnichannel Behavior

Physical store preference can coexist with digital influence before the purchase. A consumer can prefer buying in store while still being influenced by digital communication, reminders, offers, maps, availability messages, or payment benefits before the visit.

When asked what would make consumers switch from physical store to a digital channel, the answer should compare the alternative against the current channel:

- Does the digital channel preserve trust?
- Does it make product availability clear?
- Does it reduce time or effort?
- Does it keep payment simple?
- Does it provide a concrete benefit such as cashback, delivery advantage, pickup convenience, or exclusive offer?

For mature consumers, trust, clarity, and assistance tend to matter more than novelty. For younger adults, digital pre-purchase influence may work even if final purchase remains in store.

Do not assume digital migration is desirable for every segment. Compare alternatives against the preferred channel in the profile and mention uncertainty when the cluster does not strongly differentiate channel behavior.
