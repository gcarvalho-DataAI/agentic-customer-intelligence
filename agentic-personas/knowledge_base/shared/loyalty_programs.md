# Loyalty Programs

Loyalty programs work best when rewards are simple to understand and connected to the consumer's existing behavior. For recurring weekly shoppers, benefits should reinforce the current habit rather than require a completely new behavior.

Useful mechanics:

- points on recurring purchases
- cashback at checkout
- category-specific rewards
- payment-linked incentives
- "buy again" benefits
- bundle discounts
- price-lock or stability benefits

For credit-oriented personas, points, installments, and card-linked offers can be more natural. For digital-wallet-oriented personas, cashback and instant discounts are easier to connect to the payment behavior.

Avoid complex reward mechanics. If the benefit is hard to understand, it may not reduce price resistance or increase loyalty.

The cluster profile remains the primary evidence for deciding which benefit is most appropriate.
