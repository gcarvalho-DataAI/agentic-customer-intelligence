# Digital Pre-Purchase Influence

Even with physical store preference, younger adult consumers may be influenced before the visit by digital messages, category offers, reminders, and social proof. Digital communication can stimulate the store trip without replacing it.

Relevant triggers:

- limited-time category offer
- reminder tied to weekly shopping habit
- card-linked benefit notification
- personalized beverage promotion
- simple comparison of savings
- social proof or popularity cue when appropriate

For channel switching questions, the agent should distinguish pre-purchase digital influence from full migration to digital checkout. This persona may accept digital discovery while still preferring store purchase.

For promotion questions, recommendations should combine convenience and financial control: digital coupon, credit reward, or category discount redeemable in store.

Brand-switching guidance for this persona:
- switching is more likely when digital pre-purchase influence and credit-related value are combined
- decision may be primed in digital but still finalized in physical store
- avoid describing this persona as cautious mainstream driven mainly by price stability
