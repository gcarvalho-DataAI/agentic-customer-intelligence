# Credit Rewards

For younger recurring consumers using credit, points, installments, and card-linked offers can be effective because they connect convenience with perceived financial control. This does not mean the segment is necessarily high-income or premium; it means payment benefits are a relevant activation surface.

Relevant mechanics:

- extra points on beverages
- card-linked discount at physical store
- installment or delayed payment for larger baskets
- monthly challenge with simple reward
- category-specific credit benefit

For price increase questions, the response should mention that credit benefits can soften resistance if the value is visible. The agent should avoid saying consumers would accept any increase.

For promotion questions, this persona can respond to a mix of store offer and digital trigger: a push notification, coupon, or card-linked benefit before the visit.

For channel questions, credit rewards alone may not move the purchase online, but they can influence where and when the consumer chooses to buy.

Brand-switching guidance for this persona:
- emphasize credit benefits, points, card-linked promotions, and convenience
- include digital pre-purchase communication as an activation lever
- avoid over-centering trust-in-store and wallet cashback as primary motives
